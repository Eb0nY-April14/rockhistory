History of Olumo Rock.

Olumo Rock is an ancient rock located in a popular city called 'Abeokuta' which means 'a city under the Rock'. The city is situated in the south west region of Nigeria and served as a fortress to the Egba residents during the inter-tribal war around 1800. The rock was discovered by a hunter named 'Adagba' in the 19th Century and was beneficial in helping the Egba’s win the inter-tribal war.

The name 'Olumo' is derived from 2 words in Yoruba language 'Olu' which means 'God' and 'Mo' which means 'Moulded' and put together means 'God benevolently moulded this rock'. It served as a strategic hideout, a fortress and a vantage point for Egba Warriors to monitor the enemy’s advance, leading to eventual triumph in war. It housed them for 3 years while the war lasted so Olumo Rock is not just a tourist attraction but a symbol of freedom, victory, strength and peace for the Egba people.

It is a massive outcrop of granite rock which towers above the city makes it nature's gift to mankind. The complete climb without any shortcuts will take about 1 hour 30 minutes and there are man-made stairs carved into the rock for a part of the climb and afterwards you must climb with rocks as footholds along with some ladders that have been provided as an aid to visitors. There is an option of an automated lift provided for small kids and those who are afraid of heights and cannot climb the rock by themselves so they don't miss out on the adventure of experiencing what it feels like to reach the peak of the rock.

It is home to many tourists, mountain climbers and fun-lovers around the world therefore, a beehive of activites takes place there throughout the year. There are tour guides to take you on the journey to the top of the rock while explaining the history of the rock. On the way, you will find footprints of the early dwellers along with remnants of some of their belongings.

The centre boasts of a contemporary art gallery filled with paintings and sculptures, an heritage museum, the popular african tie and dye cloth market, restaurants, restrooms and free parking area.

Facts and Figures

  • The Rock has a height of 137 metres above sea level.
  • It is a source of revenue to the Ogun State government.
  • It is one of the most popular tourist attraction site in Nigeria.
  • Families come over during public holidays and festive seasons.
  • Students ranging from primary to tertiary schools come over for excursion trips and picnics.
  • There are tour guides on ground who are very knowledgeable and have great background into the history of the rock as well as the history of the ancient city of Abeokuta.
